


Area based elements: Fire, Ice, Water, Steam
	Modifying elements: Shock
	
	Levels:
		Levels are used to represent the strength of an enviormental effect
		Effects created by spells gain their level from the level of spell which was cast
	
	Effects:
		Fire: Ticking damage, changes other states of matter
		Ice: Slippery surfaces, solid surfaces, cannot add shock
		Steam: Concealment, blocks line of sight, disapears over time
		Water: Can be transformed into other area elements, puts out fire effects
		Shock: Stuns enemies periodically
		
	Elements can be changed based on level of enviormental effect or spell, for example:
		Water subtracts from fire, becoming steam:
			Level 1 water vs level 3 fire = Level 2 fire, + Level 1 steam
		Fire subtracts from water, becoming steam:
			Level 3 water vs level 1 fire = Level 1 steam + level 2 water
		Add ice and water together:
			Level 2 ice vs level 1 water = Level 3 ice
			
		Shock:
			Functions as an additional effect for existing water and steam enviormental effects.
			Stuns characters that are hit by it, potentially only slowing the player instead of stunning them
			Does not add to or subtract from the level of enviormental effects
